Video Experience Sampling Tool (VEST)

About VEST

VEST is a browser extension that utilizes a well-established research technique called "Experience Sampling" for people watching online videos. We're interested in the portrayal of technology use on television (and in movies, on YouTube, etc.), and how people react to or think about what they see in these videos. In particular, we are interested in how cyber security and privacy are shown on TV, but we hope you'll share examples of any technology use you see, even if it's not related to security or privacy. Learn more about how VEST works.

How VEST Works

VEST has three main features:

(1) Submit a report via VEST when you see something on TV, in a movie, or in a video that is related to technology use.

(2) VEST will ask you questions about what you're watching while you're watching it. You can pause/restart these questions any time you want.

(3) VEST will record all the videos you watch online. VEST only records videos you watch on a set of whitelisted sites, which you can edit. You can pause/restart this recording any time you want.

Click here to see the full tutorial and explanation, which contains all of the information that you'll see when downloading VEST before any information is collected about you.


All of these features work without collecting any identifying information about you. You can stop participating at any time by deleting the extension (find instructions for this in the full tutorial).

We're working on a couple of additional features that won't be available right away. First, we'll give you a way to submit reports and answer questions via voice/audio input -- this will be opt-in. Second, we're working on providing a way for you to see how much information you've submitted via VEST, so it won't feel like you're sending reports into a black hole.
